Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 12, 2026 · Last updated: June 12, 2026

The short version. Streamza has no user accounts and runs almost entirely on your device. We do not collect your name, email, location, contacts, or your video/audio content on our servers. Your live stream is sent directly from your device to the streaming platform you choose, and your recordings are saved only on your device. We don't sell your data and we don't use advertising trackers.

This Privacy Policy explains how the Streamza mobile application (the “App”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) handles information when you use it. By using the App, you agree to this Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Who we are

Streamza is a live streaming and screen recording application for Android, published by Probros, based in Punjab, Pakistan (“the developer”). For privacy questions, contact us at probrostraders@gmail.com. For users in the EEA/UK, the developer is the data controller for the limited processing described below.

2. Information we collect

Streamza is designed to minimize data collection. The information involved falls into these categories:

Information stored only on your device

  • Stream credentials — the RTMP/RTMPS server URL and stream key you enter are stored locally on your device so you don't have to re-type them. They are not transmitted to us.
  • App settings & preferences — your chosen resolution, bitrate, frame rate, audio options and similar preferences are stored locally on your device.
  • Your recordings — screen/camera recordings you create are saved to your device's storage (gallery). We have no access to them.

Information processed to provide the core service

  • Camera, microphone and screen content — when you stream or record, the App captures audio/video from your camera, microphone and/or screen. This content is encoded on your device and sent directly to the streaming platform you select (e.g., YouTube, Twitch), or written to a local file. It is never routed through or stored on our servers.
  • Network speed test — if you tap “Upload Speed Test”, the App uploads a small block of test data to a third-party measurement endpoint (Cloudflare) to estimate your connection speed. No personal content is included.

Purchases (when subscriptions are available)

  • Subscription / purchase data — if you buy a PRO subscription, the transaction is handled by Google Play Billing and our purchase-management provider RevenueCat. They process a purchase token and an anonymous app-user identifier to verify your subscription. We do not receive your full payment details (card numbers are handled by Google).

3. What we do not collect

  • We do not require or collect your name, email address, phone number or password (there is no account).
  • We do not collect your precise or coarse location.
  • We do not access your contacts, calendar, messages or call logs.
  • We do not use third-party advertising or cross-app tracking SDKs, and we do not sell or “share” your personal information for advertising.
  • We do not upload, view or store your stream content or recordings on our servers.

5. How information is used

  • To deliver your live stream to the platform you choose.
  • To create and save your recordings on your device.
  • To remember your settings and stream credentials between sessions (on your device).
  • To estimate your connection speed when you request it.
  • To verify and manage a PRO subscription, if you purchase one.

6. Third-party services

We keep third-party services to a minimum. The services that may receive data are:

  • Your chosen streaming platform (e.g., YouTube, Facebook, Twitch, Kick, Restream, or a Custom RTMP server). When you go live, your audio/video is sent to that platform and is subject to their privacy policy and terms. We are not affiliated with these platforms.
  • Cloudflare — used solely as the endpoint for the optional upload-speed test. See the Cloudflare Privacy Policy.
  • Google Play Billing — processes subscription purchases. See the Google Privacy Policy.
  • RevenueCat — manages and validates subscription entitlements using an anonymous identifier. See the RevenueCat Privacy Policy.

7. Storage & retention

Because your settings, stream credentials and recordings are stored locally, they remain on your device until you delete them or uninstall the App. Subscription records held by Google/RevenueCat are retained according to those providers' policies for as long as needed to manage your subscription and meet legal obligations.

8. Deleting your data

You are always in control of the on-device data:

  • Clear app data in Android Settings → Apps → Streamza → Storage → “Clear data” to remove saved settings and stream credentials.
  • Uninstall the App to remove all of its locally stored data.
  • Delete individual recordings from your device gallery at any time.

For any subscription-related data, or to make a deletion request, see our Data Deletion page or email probrostraders@gmail.com.

9. Children's privacy

Streamza is intended for a general audience and is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age of digital consent in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

10. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights over your personal data:

  • EEA/UK (GDPR): rights to access, correct, delete, restrict or object to processing, and data portability. Our legal basis for the limited processing above is performing the service you request (Art. 6(1)(b)) and our legitimate interest in operating the App (Art. 6(1)(f)).
  • California (CCPA/CPRA): rights to know, delete, correct, and to opt out of the “sale” or “sharing” of personal information. We do not sell or share personal information.

Because we hold almost no personal data, most requests can be fulfilled by clearing app data or uninstalling. To exercise any right, email probrostraders@gmail.com.

11. Security

Your stream credentials and settings are stored in the App's private storage on your device. Live streams are sent using the RTMP/RTMPS protocol provided by your platform (RTMPS is encrypted; some platforms also offer plain RTMP). No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we take reasonable measures to protect the limited data the App handles.

12. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. We will revise the “Last updated” date above and, for material changes, provide notice within the App or on this website. Continued use of the App after changes means you accept the updated Policy.
